Frequently Asked Questions

What payroll taxes does an employer pay?
Employers also pay: matching Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%), Federal Unemployment Tax (FUTA, 0.6% on first $7,000), and state unemployment insurance (SUTA, varies by state). These are in addition to the amounts withheld from employee paychecks.
What is overtime pay?
Under the FLSA, non-exempt employees must receive overtime pay at 1.5x their regular hourly rate for all hours worked over 40 in a workweek. Some states (like California) have daily overtime rules as well.
