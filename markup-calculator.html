Markup vs. Margin Explained

  • Markup is based on cost: Markup % = (Price − Cost) / Cost × 100
  • Margin is based on price: Margin % = (Price − Cost) / Price × 100
  • A 100% markup = 50% margin. A 50% markup = 33.3% margin.

Frequently Asked Questions

What markup percentage should I use?
Typical markup percentages vary by industry: retail clothing 100–200%, restaurants 200–400% on food items, software/digital products often 500–1000%+. Consider your costs, competition, and perceived value when setting markup.
Is it better to think in markup or margin?
Margin is generally more useful for profitability analysis since it relates profit to revenue. Markup is useful for pricing — you know your cost and want to add a percentage to determine price. Many retailers use markup for pricing, then track margin for profitability.
