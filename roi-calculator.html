Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good ROI for a small business?
An ROI of 15–30% annually is generally considered good for a small business. Higher-risk investments should yield higher ROI to justify the risk. Compare to your cost of capital to assess whether an investment is truly worthwhile.
What is the difference between ROI and annualized ROI?
ROI measures the total return over the entire investment period. Annualized ROI (CAGR) converts this to an equivalent yearly rate, making it easy to compare investments with different time horizons.
What are the limitations of ROI?
ROI does not account for the time value of money, risk, or opportunity cost. For complex investment decisions, consider using Net Present Value (NPV) or Internal Rate of Return (IRR) alongside ROI.
