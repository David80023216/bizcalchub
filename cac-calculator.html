What is CAC?

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) = Total Marketing & Sales Spend ÷ Number of New Customers. Compare CAC to LTV to assess business health — a healthy LTV:CAC ratio is 3:1 or higher.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good CAC for my business?
There is no universal "good" CAC — it depends entirely on your customer lifetime value (LTV). The key metric is the LTV:CAC ratio. A ratio of 3:1 (LTV is 3x the CAC) is generally considered healthy. Under 1:1 means you are losing money on every customer.
How can I reduce my CAC?
Strategies to reduce CAC include: improving conversion rates on your website, investing in organic SEO and content marketing, leveraging referral programs, refining audience targeting, and optimizing your sales process to close deals faster.
