How to Calculate Profit Margin

  • Gross Margin % = (Revenue − COGS) / Revenue × 100
  • Net Margin % = (Revenue − COGS − Expenses) / Revenue × 100
  • Markup % = (Selling Price − Cost) / Cost × 100
  • To find selling price: Price = Cost / (1 − Desired Margin%)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good profit margin for a small business?
A net profit margin of 10% is generally considered average, 20% is good, and 30%+ is excellent. However, margins vary widely by industry. Retail businesses often have margins of 2–5%, while software companies may exceed 70%.
What is the difference between profit margin and markup?
Profit margin is calculated as a percentage of the selling price (revenue), while markup is calculated as a percentage of the cost. A 50% markup equals a 33.3% margin. Always clarify which metric you are using when discussing pricing.
What is the difference between gross and net profit margin?
Gross margin only subtracts the direct cost of goods sold (COGS) from revenue. Net margin subtracts all expenses including operating costs, taxes, and interest. Net margin gives a fuller picture of overall profitability.
