Burn Rate Explained

  • Gross Burn Rate = Total monthly expenses (before revenue)
  • Net Burn Rate = Monthly expenses − Monthly revenue
  • Runway = Current Cash ÷ Net Burn Rate
  • Most investors recommend maintaining 18–24 months of runway

Frequently Asked Questions

How much runway should a startup have?
Most startup advisors recommend maintaining at least 18 months of runway. This gives enough time to hit milestones, raise your next round, or pivot. Starting a fundraise with less than 6 months of runway is very risky.
How can I extend my startup runway?
Options include: cutting non-essential expenses, delaying hires, negotiating better vendor terms, increasing revenue, raising bridge funding, or applying for grants, SBIR funding, or revenue-based financing.
What is a typical startup burn rate?
Burn rates vary enormously. Early-stage pre-revenue startups often burn $10,000–$50,000/month. Funded startups with teams may burn $100,000–$500,000+/month. The key is always the runway relative to your next funding milestone.
